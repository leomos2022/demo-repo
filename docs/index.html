French Potato Gratin Dauphinoise

French Potato Gratin Dauphinoise, also known as Gratin Dauphinois, is a classic French dish that originated in the Dauphiné region of France. The dish is typically made with thinly sliced potatoes that are layered in a baking dish and baked in a mixture of cream and garlic until they are tender and golden brown. The dish has been a popular staple of French cuisine for many years, and it is often served as a side dish to accompany a variety of main dishes. The origins of the dish are somewhat unclear, but it is believed to have been created in the early 18th century by a French chef named Nicolas Béchameil. Despite its humble origins, French Potato Gratin Dauphinoise has become a beloved dish both in France and around the world.
